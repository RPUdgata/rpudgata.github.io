  • Project 1: Black-body Radiation and its Applications in Cosmology

      • This report delves into the elegant application of Wien's Displacement Law to ascertain the surface temperatures of A to K stars, while also exploring the inherent limitations associated with this method. The study encompassed meticulous measurement of the spectra of these celestial bodies, while duly considering atmospheric and instrumental factors, with the aim of identifying the wavelengths at which their intensity reached the zenith. By adeptly employing Wien's law, the subsequent determination of surface temperatures became possible. The findings revealed a commendable alignment for F to early K stars, whereas A and later K stars exhibited substandard accord, primarily due to deviations from the ideal black-body spectra and the constraints posed by the measurement equipment in capturing the peak intensities at profound red and blue wavelengths.

Overall, these findings contribute valuable insights for policymakers, investors, and stakeholders in promoting sustainable energy practices and mitigating environmental challenges.

  • Project 3: Fabrication of Two-Dimensional (2D) Semiconducting Layers and its Electronic Properties

      • This study presents a comprehensive analysis of SnS2, a promising material with potential applications in electronics, optoelectronics, and energy conversion systems. Through the use of various analytical techniques, including Scanning Electron Miscroscopy (SEM), Transmission Electron Miscroscopy (TEM), Raman spectroscopy, X-ray Diffraction (XRD), and UV-Vis analysis, a detailed understanding of SnS2's structural, vibrational, and optical properties was achieved. SEM analysis revealed the hexagonal shape of SnS2, while TEM provided insights into its crystal structure, grain boundaries, defects, and nanoscale features. Raman spectroscopy offered information on the vibrational modes and bonding characteristics of SnS2. XRD analysis, in conjunction with Bragg's Law, enabled the determination of the lattice constant. Additionally, UV-Vis analysis using the Tauc plot method yielded the bandgap energy of SnS2. These findings contribute to a comprehensive understanding of SnS2's properties, facilitating its further exploration and optimization for electronic applications. Overall, this study provides valuable insights into SnS2's potential as a versatile material in various technological fields.

  • Publication 1: A Comparative Study on the Efficiency of Nickel Oxide/Polyaniline and Cobalt(II,III) Oxide/Polyaniline toward Oxygen Reduction Reaction

      • Polyaniline (PANI) is a conducting polymer that has gained attention as a matrix material for oxygen reduction reaction (ORR) catalysts due to its high surface area, good electrical conductivity, and stability. In this comparative study, the electrochemical properties of nickel oxide (NiO) and cobalt (II,III) oxide (Co3O4) catalysts, both embedded in a PANI matrix, were investigated and compared for ORR. The NiO and Co3O4 catalysts were synthesized using a simple reduction method and embedded in the PANI matrix through mechanical mixing. The synthesized catalysts were characterized using X-ray diffraction, Fouriertransform infrared spectroscopy, and ultraviolet (UV)-visible spectroscopy. The electrochemical performance of the NiO/PANI and Co3O4/PANI catalysts was evaluated using cyclic voltammetry and electrochemical impedance spectroscopy. The results showed that NiO/PANI catalyst possessed slightly higher activity than the Co3O4/PANI catalyst. The stability of the catalysts was also investigated from a cyclic voltammogram, where the catalysts were cycled between −0.7 V and 1.5 V for 100 cycles. The results showed that the NiO/PANI and Co3O4/PANI catalysts both exhibited good stability. Overall, the comparative study demonstrated that both NiO/PANI and Co3O4/PANI catalysts exhibited good electrocatalytic activity and stability for ORR. The study also showed that the PANI matrix provided stable and conductive support for the catalysts. The results suggest that both NiO and Co3O4, embedded in a PANI matrix, have the potential to be used as low-cost and efficient catalysts for ORR.
